Encouraging More Women

Six Suggestions to encouage more women to join STEM fields

We need to change the current situation that female does not have equal represenation in STEM majors. If we want to attract the best and brightest minds into the fields, we have to consider all of the population. More women can contribute to our field, and we can make that happen. In the following sections, I proposed 5 suggestions to increase the female representation.

Understanding Bias

The very first step of resolving this issue is to letting more people to understand the existance and the importance of the matter. This website aims to broadcast it, hoping to attract more attention from the society and the educational institution.

Provide more resources to younge girls

At early stage of the education, we should encouage girls to join STEM programs by offering more resources, such as female STEM club, female STEM program, female STEM activities. We should provide mentorship for those who are interested to continue pursuing their goal and dream. Although focusing on one female age group, would not resolve this sociological issue, we can at least correcting people's false assumptions that girls are not good at math and science. The school and administrators should also aim to create an equal learning and studying environment for younge girls.Providing equal opportunities for girls and boys may alleviate this issue.

Enrich learning opportunities from the community

National clubs are paying more attention to women students in STEM. A new club has been established in 2017 called Women in STEM to unit and assist girls in STEM majors. Tech giant company, such as google,initilize programs that targted female engineers to increase job opportunities in the cloud computing industry during the summer. Many firms also offers job shadowing programs or career days that gives potential female employees an opportunity to observe typical daily job duties and actiities.

Serving as Mentor

One big problems of this issue is the lack of female mentor. Mentor is like a lighthouse in the sea, offering suggestions and assistant. Therefore, we encouarge more success female figures to serve student mentor. They should be not only willing to spend their time teaching techniques and processes, but also interested in long-term development. Working with a mentor is a career strategy that can bring significant benefits and we courage more and more female students to look for one.

Leaky Pipline

The "leaky pipeline" metaphor describes the way in which women become underrepresented minorities in the STEM fields. In mathematics and engineering the disequilibrium starts before undergrad, with fewer women choosing to major in these fields. Conversely, women in the biological and life sciences represent more than half of the students earning PhDs, yet most fail to achieve tenure track faculty positions in academia. -- Amber Mueller
