Current Situation

What is STEM? Why it is important? What are current figures?

STEM

STEM refers to four fields: Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics. The National Science Foundation estimates that about five million people work in related fields. Many Science and engineering occupations are predicted to grow faster than the average rate of all occupations. Some of the largest increases will be in engineering and computer related fields. However, women currently hold only one-quarter of the positions. (Lacey & Wright, 2009; National Science Board, 2010).

Little Interest in Secondary School

The figure on the left shows the gender differences in subject choice in secondary school. Male students are three times more likely to study physics and mathematics. There are also a huge gap in subjects such as engineering, construction, design graphics and technology.

Imbalanced Gender Representation in College

Only 7% of women who graduated from college earned a STEM degree. STEM degrees are more popular with men. About 15% of men who graduated in 2016 earned a STEM degree. The breakdown of STEM graduates in 2016 was 37% female and 63% male.

It's imporant for women to participant.

Since 1970, the representation of women has increased across all STEM occupations and they made significant gains in social science occupations. Women in 2019 also made up nearly half of those in all math (47%) and life and physical science (45%) occupations. However, women did not make as big gains in computer and engineering occupations, which made up the largest portion (80%) of the STEM workforce.

Leaky Pipline

The "leaky pipeline" metaphor describes the way in which women become underrepresented minorities in the STEM fields. In mathematics and engineering the disequilibrium starts before undergrad, with fewer women choosing to major in these fields. Conversely, women in the biological and life sciences represent more than half of the students earning PhDs, yet most fail to achieve tenure track faculty positions in academia. -- Amber Mueller
