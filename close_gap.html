Closing Confidence Gap

There is a wide-believed rumor that boys are good at math while girls don't in elemenetray school. According to a research a journal named Girls' and Boys' Mathematics Achievement, Affect, and Experiences: Findings from ECLS-K, many girls lose confidence in math by third grade. Boys, on the other hand, are more likely to say they are strong in math by 2nd grade, before any performance differences are evident. With more women joining the STEM areas, girls at early ages would be more confident studying math and science courses, which helps them success in the future. A positive feedback loop is needed for girls and female students.
